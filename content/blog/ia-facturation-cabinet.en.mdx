In a professional firm, billing is rarely the visible problem. It's the invisible one: time logged late, services never invoiced, follow-ups postponed because there's no bandwidth, month-end surprises where the pipeline looks full but cash is tight.

Billing automation isn't about replacing your accountant or office manager. It's about eliminating the mechanical tasks that drag on their work — and yours.

---

## The billing cycle: where time and money disappear

### Time tracking

In firms that bill by the hour, time tracking is the first source of loss. Team members log what they did at the end of the day — sometimes at the end of the week. Gaps accumulate. Internal studies across several consulting firms show that 12 to 18% of actual work time is never invoiced.

AI can capture time automatically from the tools already in use: calendars, emails, tickets, open documents. No manual entry, no forgotten tasks.

### Invoice generation

Creating an invoice sounds simple. In practice, it means assembling data from multiple sources: logged hours, rates by team member, contract terms, applicable VAT, client details. Each step is a chance for error.

**Automated invoice generation** pulls that data from your CRM, time tracking tool, and contracts. The invoice is built, checked, and ready to send — with no re-entry.

### Payment reminders

Manual follow-ups are time-consuming and uncomfortable. So they get delayed. Average payment terms in French SMBs exceed 50 days. In professional firms, it's often longer.

An **automated reminder system** sends the right message at the right time: a nudge at day 15, a follow-up at day 30, an internal alert at day 45. Tone is customizable, tracking is centralized, and no one has to remember to do it.

### Revenue recognition

For firms running long engagements — consulting, audit, annual retainers — revenue recognition is a delicate accounting exercise. When do you recognize revenue? Over which period?

AI can automate this calculation by linking project milestones to predefined accounting rules, and generating the corresponding entries in your accounting system.

## How to deploy billing automation

### Step 1: Map your current cycle

Before automating, understand where the friction is. How long between completing an engagement and issuing the invoice? What's your invoice correction rate? What's your DSO (days sales outstanding)?

These metrics are your **baseline** for measuring gains.

### Step 2: Choose the right entry points

Automating everything at once rarely works. Firms that succeed start with something simple — usually payment reminders, which require no complex integration and deliver fast, visible results.

Invoice generation comes next, then automated time capture.

### Step 3: Connect your existing tools

Billing automation only works if it talks to your stack: your accounting software (Sage, Cegid, QuickBooks), your CRM, your project management tool. Connectors exist for most common setups.

For firms without a structured stack, this is also an opportunity to build a solid foundation.

## What changes in practice

- **Faster invoicing**: from 10-15 days after engagement to under 48 hours
- **Better collections**: automated reminders reduce DSO by 20 to 35%
- **Zero forgotten services**: automatic time capture eliminates billing leakage
- **Less month-end stress**: billing becomes a continuous flow, not a sprint

---

## Mistakes to avoid

**Automating without cleaning your data**: if your client database is incomplete or rates are wrong, automation just generates incorrect invoices faster. Start with a data audit.

**Skipping human validation**: automation doesn't mean zero review. Set validation rules for invoices above a certain threshold, or for engagements with non-standard terms.

**Generic reminder copy**: a long-standing client isn't followed up the same way as a new one. Modern systems make segmentation straightforward.

For most firms, that's DSO and follow-ups. It's the fastest entry point with the most visible ROI.
