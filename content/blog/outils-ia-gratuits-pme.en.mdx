**TL;DR**: There's a handful of genuinely useful free AI tools for SMBs — not just timed trials. This guide separates the honest freemium offerings from the ones too restricted to matter in daily work.

---

Good news: you don't need a budget to start with AI. Many tools offer free plans that are genuinely usable beyond the demo phase. Bad news: not all of them. Some free tiers are so restrictive they exist only to make you want to pay.

This guide tells you exactly what you get — and what you can't do — on each free plan.

---

## General-Purpose AI Assistants: Free Access

### ChatGPT Free (OpenAI)

ChatGPT Free gives access to **GPT-4o mini**, a capable and fast model. It's honestly sufficient for:

- Drafting and rephrasing emails
- Preparing presentations or summaries
- Answering common business questions
- Generating content or campaign ideas

**What you can't do on the free plan**:
- Access to the o3 model (advanced reasoning)
- Unlimited real-time web browsing
- Image generation (DALL·E)
- Priority access to new features

**Main limit**: you can hit generation caps with heavy use. For moderate daily use (10–20 exchanges/day), the free plan holds up fine.

**Verdict**: An excellent entry point. Start here.

### Claude Free (Anthropic)

Claude's free plan gives access to **Claude 3.5 Haiku** and limited access to **Claude 3.7 Sonnet** (the most capable model). Volume limits are tighter than ChatGPT Free, but output quality — especially for structured writing — is often better.

**What you can do**:
- High-quality structured writing
- Document analysis (file uploads supported)
- Long conversations with good coherence

**What you can't do**:
- Web browsing
- Unlimited access to the most powerful models
- Projects features (shared team memory)

**Verdict**: Ideal if writing quality and consistency are your top priority. Volume limits will push you toward the paid plan fairly quickly.

### Mistral Le Chat (Free)

Mistral is a European alternative with a free plan that gives access to competitive models. A meaningful advantage: **data hosted in Europe**, which simplifies GDPR compliance.

**What you can do**:
- Strong writing quality in multiple languages (native multilingual model)
- Web browsing included on the free plan
- File upload and document analysis

**Verdict**: Strongly recommended for businesses where data residency in Europe matters. Less known, consistently underestimated.

## Free Productivity and Automation Tools

### Make (Free Plan)

Make (formerly Integromat) lets you build automations between your apps without code. The free plan includes **1,000 operations per month** and up to 2 active scenarios.

**What you can automate for free**:
- Send a Slack notification when a specific email arrives
- Automatically add a contact to your CRM from a form submission
- Sync data between two tools

**Main limit**: 1,000 operations/month runs out fast if you're automating recurring processes. A Starter plan (~$10/month) quickly removes this constraint.

**Verdict**: Perfect for testing your first automations before committing budget.

### n8n (Open-Source, Self-Hosted)

n8n is the open-source alternative to Make and Zapier. It's **free if you host it yourself** (on a server or VPS). Advantages: no operation limits, full data control.

**Best for**: SMBs with some technical capacity or an IT partner. Not for fully non-technical teams without support.

**Verdict**: Excellent value for SMBs with technical resources, or those working with an AI implementation partner.

## Free Meeting and Transcription Tools

### Fathom (Free Plan)

Fathom records, transcribes, and summarizes your Zoom, Teams, or Meet calls. The free plan offers **unlimited recordings and summaries** — no time or volume cap.

**What you get for free**:
- Automatic transcription of all your meetings
- Structured summaries with action items
- Shareable clips of key moments

**What's paid**: advanced features (CRM sync, team access, analytics).

**Verdict**: One of the genuinely generous free plans in the AI space. Install it immediately.

### Otter.ai (Free Plan)

Otter transcribes meetings and generates summaries. The free plan offers **300 minutes of transcription per month**, enough for 3 to 5 weekly meetings.

**Verdict**: Good for getting started. Fathom is more generous if you have a high meeting volume.

## Free Design and Creation Tools

### Canva with AI (Free Plan)

Canva includes AI features on its free plan: **Magic Write** (text generation), **Magic Erase** (object removal from images), and smart templates.

**What you can do for free**:
- Create professional visuals for social media
- Generate short copy for your designs
- Access thousands of templates

**Verdict**: Essential for SMBs without a designer. The free version covers most everyday needs comfortably.

### Gamma (Free Plan)

Gamma generates complete presentations from a prompt. The free plan allows **10 creations per month**, sufficient for occasional needs.

**Verdict**: Useful for occasional client or internal presentations. Quickly limiting with regular use.

## What Free Plans Consistently Don't Cover

To be honest about the gaps: here's what free plans generally can't give you reliably.

- **Data privacy**: on most free plans, your data may be used for model training. For any sensitive data — clients, HR, financials — you need a paid plan with contractual commitments.
- **Reliability at scale**: free plans have volume caps that can block your team at the worst moment.
- **Support**: in case of problems, support is limited or absent.
- **Advanced integrations**: CRM, ERP, or API connectors are typically reserved for paid plans.

## When Should You Upgrade to a Paid Plan?

Clear signals to watch for:

- You're hitting volume limits more than twice a month
- You're starting to enter client or HR data into the tool
- The tool becomes critical to a business or operational process
- More than three people are using it regularly

Moving from a free to a paid AI plan typically costs between $12 and $35 per user per month — an investment that pays off quickly if the tool is the right fit.
