**TL;DR**: Not all SMBs start from the same place with AI. Some industries are ahead, others face structural lag. Understanding where your sector stands helps you calibrate expectations, prioritize investments, and set realistic benchmarks for your own results.

---

## Why Sector Benchmarks Matter

Comparing your AI progress to "the SMB average" doesn't tell you much. An industrial supply company and a consulting firm have completely different data assets, processes, and digital maturity levels.

Sector benchmarks give you three concrete things:
- A realistic reference point
- Priority use cases already validated by peers in your industry
- An internal persuasion tool ("our competitors are already doing this")

---

## Ahead of the Curve: AI Adoption > 40%

### Professional Services (consulting, accounting, legal)
These sectors adopted AI quickly, driven by high-volume document work.

**Dominant use cases:**
- Document drafting and synthesis (proposals, reports, contracts)
- Automated research and regulatory monitoring
- Email processing and client communication triage

**Typical ROI:** 20 to 35% productivity gain on document-heavy tasks within the first 3 months.

**Maturity indicator:** Advanced firms use AI to automate new business qualification and standardized proposal generation.

---

### E-commerce and Retail
Retail adopted AI early due to volume pressure and pricing competition.

**Dominant use cases:**
- Product recommendation personalization
- Demand forecasting and inventory management
- Automated customer service (chatbots, returns qualification)

**Typical ROI:** 15 to 25% reduction in stockouts, 10 to 20% increase in average cart value through personalization.

**Maturity indicator:** Leading players use AI for real-time dynamic pricing.

---

## Catching Up: AI Adoption 20-40%

### Manufacturing and Industry
Manufacturing is often slowed by legacy systems and non-digitized data. But the gap is closing quickly.

**Dominant use cases:**
- Predictive maintenance (sensors + AI analysis)
- Computer vision-assisted quality control
- Optimized production scheduling

**Typical ROI:** 10 to 30% reduction in unplanned downtime through predictive maintenance.

**Main blocker:** Data quality is frequently insufficient. Industrial SMBs underestimate the data preparation work required before any AI project.

---

### Healthcare and Personal Services
Regulation slows adoption, but administrative use cases are gaining ground.

**Dominant use cases:**
- Administrative task automation (billing, scheduling)
- Report and clinical note drafting assistance
- Staff scheduling and availability management

**Typical ROI:** 15 to 25% reduction in administrative time for clinical staff.

**Maturity indicator:** Advanced organizations are beginning to use AI for clinical decision support within strictly governed frameworks.

---

### Construction and Real Estate
A traditionally slow sector now accelerating rapidly since 2024.

**Dominant use cases:**
- Document management and site tracking
- Quote and estimate generation
- Regulatory monitoring (standards, permits)

**Typical ROI:** 30 to 50% time savings on quote production and document management.

---

## Structural Laggards: AI Adoption < 20%

### Agriculture and Food Processing
High variability depending on farm or operation size. Smaller structures often lack baseline digital readiness.

**Main blockers:** Connectivity, sensor costs, low data culture.

**Emerging opportunities:** Weather-based input planning, traceability and regulatory reporting.

---

### Skilled Trades and Local Commerce
Artisans and micro-SMBs are the furthest behind — but also stand to gain the most from basic administrative automation.

**Recommended first step:** Automate quote management, payment follow-ups, and basic bookkeeping before targeting more complex AI use cases.

## Building Your Own Benchmark

Instead of comparing yourself to generic numbers, build an actionable benchmark in 3 steps:

**1. Identify your 3 most time-consuming processes**
That's where AI delivers the fastest impact. Run an [AI self-assessment](/blog/auto-diagnostic-ia) to map them.

**2. Find peers who've already made progress**
Your industry network, trade associations, and published case studies are your best sources.

**3. Define your own success metrics**
Don't copy others' KPIs. Adapt them to your context. See [measuring AI ROI in SMBs](/blog/mesurer-roi-ia-pme).

---

## What Benchmarks Don't Tell You

Adoption and ROI figures should be read carefully:

- **Adoption isn't maturity**: having a ChatGPT subscription isn't the same as integrating AI into business workflows.
- **ROI varies enormously** based on implementation quality, not just industry.
- **"Lagging" can be an opportunity**: in less advanced sectors, early movers have a significant competitive edge.

See our analysis of [AI trends for SMBs in 2026](/blog/ia-pme-2026-tendances) for a full forward-looking view.

---

## The Right Way to Read Benchmarks

Don't look at benchmarks to find out if you're "behind." Use them to identify **use cases that have already been proven** in your sector — and start there. That's the most reliable path to fast ROI.
